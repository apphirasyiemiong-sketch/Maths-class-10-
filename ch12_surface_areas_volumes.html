Important Notes & Formulas

Key concepts extracted from Exercises 12.1 and 12.2

🔷 Cube

Surface Area = 6a²
Volume = a³
Diagonal = a√3

📦 Cuboid

Surface Area = 2(lb + bh + hl)
Volume = l × b × h
Diagonal = √(l²+b²+h²)

🥫 Cylinder

CSA = 2πrh
TSA = 2πr(r + h)
Volume = πr²h

🔺 Cone

CSA = πrl
TSA = πr(r + l)
Volume = ⅓πr²h
l = √(h² + r²)

🌍 Sphere

Surface Area = 4πr²
Volume = ⁴⁄₃πr³

🌓 Hemisphere

CSA = 2πr²
TSA = 3πr²
Volume = ⅔πr³

⚡ Key Concepts from Exercises

  • Joining Solids: When two solids are joined end-to-end, the joining faces disappear from the total surface area.
  • Volume of Combinations: Simply add the volumes of individual constituent solids.
  • Surface Area of Combinations: Add the curved surface areas only. Subtract the area of the common/joining base.
  • Depressions/Cavities: When a depression is cut out, subtract the volume of the cutout. For surface area, subtract the base area of cutout but add the CSA of the depression.
  • Hollow Vessels: Calculate inner surface area considering only interior surfaces (cylinder CSA + hemisphere CSA).
  • Displacement Method: Volume of water displaced = Volume of submerged object.
  • π Value: Use π = 22/7 unless stated otherwise. For some problems, π = 3.14 is specified.
